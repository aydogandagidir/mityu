FAQ

Fair questions.

Is my audio uploaded anywhere?

No. Recording, transcription, and storage happen on your device, and the app works with the network unplugged. Data leaves your machine only if you explicitly configure a cloud model with your own API key — and even then it is transcript text, never audio. Analytics are off unless you opt in and never include meeting content; opt-in redaction can scrub names, numbers, and emails.

Does Mityu join my calls as a bot?

No. It captures the audio already playing on your computer plus your microphone. Nothing joins the meeting and nothing appears in the participant list. A visible indicator is always on while recording — following local consent rules when you record others is on you.

Which platforms are supported?

Windows 10/11 and macOS 13+ with full system-audio capture (macOS asks once for the screen-recording permission — that is how the OS gates system audio). Linux is experimental and microphone-only for now.

Does it really work offline?

Yes — fully. Record, transcribe, summarize with local models, search, and export with no connection at all. You only need internet for optional cloud models and update checks.

Which AI models does it use — do I need an API key?

No key required. Transcription runs locally with Whisper large-v3 or NVIDIA Parakeet; summaries run locally via Ollama or the built-in pipeline. Prefer a cloud model? Bring your own key for OpenAI, Anthropic, Groq, or OpenRouter — keys are stored in your OS keychain, never in plaintext.

What is the refund policy?

Every purchase includes a 14-day money-back guarantee. If Mityu is not for you, email support within 14 days for a full refund — no questions asked.
